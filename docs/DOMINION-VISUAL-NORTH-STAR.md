# Dominion visual north star

The approved cinematic render is the canonical design target for Dominion AI. The live interface
must read as a premium physical command console, not a conventional web dashboard with sci-fi
colors.

## Non-negotiable qualities

- The processor and radiating circuitry are the visual hero and remain clearly visible.
- Dark titanium, thick smoked glass, copper conductors, and green energy behave as materials and
  shared environmental light—not independent CSS colors.
- Header, archive, telemetry, conversation, and composer read as one machined assembly.
- Frames have mass: bevels, recessed cavities, edge absorption, bright specular catches, and deep
  shadows. Thin one-pixel rectangles alone are not an acceptable substitute.
- Green light is localized to energy, state, and activity. Copper is a reflective metal accent.
- Icons remain large, legible, custom, and housed in dimensional controls.
- Messages are command records and analysis modules, not rounded messenger bubbles.
- Motion depicts optical or electrical behavior: glare, caustics, refraction, energy travel, and
  press depth. It must also respect reduced-motion preferences.
- Desktop should feel cinematic and dense. Mobile may rearrange or omit secondary telemetry but
  must preserve the same materials, lighting, and authority.

## Fidelity rejection checks

Reject a visual change if it makes the interface look flatter, hides the circuitry, shrinks or dims
the icon system, replaces material depth with generic gradients, turns the glass into simple
transparency, or moves the model selector out of the command rail. Existing application features
and operating modes remain intact unless a separate product decision explicitly changes them.
